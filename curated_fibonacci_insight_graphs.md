## Biblical-theological ethos note

This document should be read with the repository's sparse but governing biblical-theological ethos in view: the Fibonacci `1` may serve, in metaphor, as a witness to the same God before creation and after creation. The second `1` is not the world itself; it is the same `1` received from the world's side, the first known touchpoint of God as Creator and Sustainer, a `1` unto others. Recurrence then becomes a practical handle for how created processes begin, differentiate, carry memory, live with tension, receive correction, and are drawn toward faithful return and tensionlessness. Keep this ethos present without letting it override exact mathematics, source hierarchy, Scripture, or evidence-boundary cautions.

## Editorial result

- **25** graph concepts retained from the original 165-item draft.
- **50** new graph concepts added—exactly twice the retained count.
- **75** total graphs, alphabetized below.

The selection favors graphs that reveal recurrence, convergence, exact identities, geometric construction, information structure, empirical testability, and the proposed time metaphor. It removes redundant chart-style variants, document-format counts, image-audit mechanics, and pixel/rendering diagnostics that do not materially deepen understanding of the Fibonacci family.

> **Interpretive boundary:** Fibonacci identities and convergence laws are mathematical facts. Natural, financial, human-proportion, metadata, and time applications must be tested or explicitly treated as models. The mapping yesterday = 1/φ, today = 1, tomorrow = φ is a useful metaphorical coordinate system; it is not a physical law or a claim that future events are literally predetermined.

## Fifteen gateway graphs

These are the best first sequence for teaching the subject from recurrence to implication:

1. **Destination Addition Decomposition** — Shows every term as the visible sum of its two known predecessors—the most fundamental recurrence picture.
2. **Recurrence Dependency Graph** — Makes 'known from known' explicit by drawing every term as dependent on the prior two.
3. **Canonical Destination Log Plot** — Turns near-exponential growth into an almost straight line and exposes the small early deviations.
4. **Forward Ratio Line Chart** — The primary convergence chart: consecutive ratios oscillate toward φ.
5. **Forward Ratio Alternating Envelope** — Separates odd and even ratios to make the tightening upper and lower bounds around φ obvious.
6. **Signed Error Alternation Plot** — Preserves direction as well as magnitude, exposing the exact alternating decay around φ.
7. **Consecutive-Pair Scatter Plot** — Plots (Fₙ,Fₙ₊₁) against y=φx so the sequence visibly approaches the golden direction.
8. **Source-Pair Contribution Share** — Shows the two prior terms contributing approximately 1/φ and 1/φ² of the next term.
9. **Cassini Identity Alternation** — Plots Fₙ₊₁Fₙ₋₁−Fₙ²=(-1)ⁿ, revealing a permanent ±1 invariant inside enormous numbers.
10. **Ratio Recurrence Cobweb Diagram** — Iterates rₙ₊₁=1+1/rₙ and shows convergence to the positive fixed point φ.
11. **Zeckendorf Decomposition Map** — Shows every positive integer as a unique sum of nonconsecutive Fibonacci numbers.
12. **Golden-Angle Rotation Map** — Plots repeated rotations by 360°/φ²≈137.5°, showing why points distribute evenly around a circle.
13. **Empirical Spiral Fit: Golden vs Alternatives** — Compares residual error for a logarithmic golden spiral, a Fibonacci quarter-circle spiral, and competing spiral models.
14. **Normalized Time-Reference Number Line** — Places yesterday=1/φ, today=1, and tomorrow=φ on one shared coordinate system.
15. **Reciprocal Time Mirror** — Uses x↦1/x to exchange yesterday=1/φ and tomorrow=φ while fixing today=1.

## Source and lens key

- **Retained:** selected from the original 165-name inventory.
- **New:** introduced in this refined edition.
- **Math / Geometry:** directly computed from Fibonacci numbers, φ, identities, or geometric constructions.
- **Metadata / systems:** requires document coding, workflow data, or an information-system use case.
- **Reality test:** requires measured external observations and should compare Fibonacci claims with alternatives.
- **Time metaphor:** uses 1/φ, 1, and φ as interpretive temporal coordinates.

## What was intentionally left out

The removed material primarily consisted of:

- multiple cosmetic renderings of the same data question, such as line, bar, lollipop, and step versions of one series;
- document typography, page, paragraph, table, and word-count graphs;
- screenshot, antialiasing, marker-size, line-width, and export-resolution diagnostics;
- checklist completion, image pass/fail, and missing-label audit graphs;
- generic inventory counts that describe the packet but do not explain Fibonacci structure.

Those graphs may still be useful for quality assurance, but they are not part of this conceptual canon.
