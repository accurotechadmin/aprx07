This document should be read with the repository's sparse but governing biblical-theological ethos in view: the first Fibonacci `1` may serve, in metaphor, as witness to God before creation, while the second `1` is not the world but the world's receiving-end view of the same God as Creator and Sustainer, the first known touchpoint of His breath. Keep this ethos present without letting it override exact mathematics, source hierarchy, Scripture, or evidence-boundary cautions.

## 2. Source hierarchy canon

Use this authority order whenever sources feel redundant or memory becomes saturated:

1. **Truth Inventory (`TR`)**: positive facts are highest authority.
2. **Avoids Inventory (`AV`) and reference appendix (`REF`)**: failure taxonomy plus canonical Fv -1 through Fv 28 destinations.
3. **Insight-graph suite and drawing guide**: operational standards for selecting and producing visualizations under the truth/avoid rules.
4. **Detailed Description**: crosswalk and structure guide.
5. **Cohesion Audit and Statement/Claim Index**: verification and traceability aids.
6. **Glossary and Glossary Seed**: terminology aids and provenance.
7. **Comprehensive Report and Boot-Up Primer**: synthesis and workflow framing.
8. **Curated graph files and spreadsheet**: applied graph selection and extended data, subordinate to the governing convention.

Never treat supplemental synthesis, glossary prose, graph lists, or spreadsheet labels as overriding explicit TR, AV, or REF statements.

## 3. Governing Fv convention canon

Use the repository's Fv process-step convention unless a task explicitly declares another indexing system and converts it:

- `Fv -1 -> destination 0`
- `Fv 0 -> destination 1`
- `Fv 1 -> destination 1`
- every later destination is obtained by adding the two immediately preceding destinations.

Let `Fv v` name the process step and `D_v` name the destination value at that step. Under conventional Fibonacci indexing with `F_0 = 0` and `F_1 = 1`, the conversion is:

`D_v = F_(v+1)`.

Never silently omit the zero at Fv -1, merge the duplicate ones at Fv 0 and Fv 1, shift a destination to another step, or mix conventional `F_n` labels with Fv labels without an explicit conversion.

## 4A. Near/mid/far range canon

Whenever a Fibonacci sequence is shown as more than a tiny example, default to three explicitly named horizons:

- **Near horizon: steps 1-8** for introductory, seed-visible, compact recurrence examples.
- **Mid horizon: steps 1-30** for complete reference-grade work; under the repository Fv convention, identify whether this means the canonical REF/TR range `Fv -1` through `Fv 28` or a converted conventional step-1-through-step-30 list.
- **Far horizon: steps 1-100** for extended convergence, scale, late-ratio, rounding, log-view, and spreadsheet-supported analysis. Values beyond Fv 28 are recurrence-computed extensions unless another source is named.

If another range better fits the question, state the reason and mark the range explicitly. Do not let a partial range look like the complete sequence or complete reference.

## 5. Ratio and limit canon

Forward ratio:

`R_v = D_v / D_(v-1)`, defined for `v >= 1` under the stated seeds.

Reciprocal ratio:

`Q_v = D_(v-1) / D_v`, defined for `v >= 1` under the stated seeds.

Limits and identities:

- `phi = (1 + sqrt(5)) / 2 = 1.618033988749895...`
- `1/phi = phi - 1 = 0.618033988749895...`
- `R_v` approaches `phi`.
- `Q_v` approaches `1/phi`.
- `R_(v+1) = 1 + 1/R_v`.
- `R_(v+1) - phi = -(R_v - phi)/(phi * R_v)`.
- The sign of `R_v - phi` alternates beginning with `R_1 < phi` and `R_2 > phi`.
- The magnitude `|R_v - phi|` decreases.
- The ratio of successive error magnitudes approaches `1/phi^2`; it is not exactly that multiplier at every finite step.
- Every finite forward or reciprocal Fibonacci ratio is rational.
- No finite forward ratio equals `phi` exactly.
- No finite reciprocal ratio equals `1/phi` exactly.

Use `=` only for exact identities, `≈` for rounded decimals or measurements, and words like “approaches” for limits.

## 6. Time metaphor canon

The Yesterday/Today/Tomorrow mapping is a declared interpretive metaphor, not a Fibonacci theorem and not a physical law:

- **Yesterday** = `1/phi ≈ 0.618033988750`
- **Today** = `1`
- **Tomorrow** = `phi ≈ 1.618033988750`

Do not place Yesterday on a forward-ratio limit curve or Tomorrow on a reciprocal-ratio limit curve. Do not imply these three are equally spaced on a linear axis:

- `1 - 1/phi = 1/phi^2 ≈ 0.381966`
- `phi - 1 = 1/phi ≈ 0.618034`
- the future-side interval is `phi` times the past-side interval.

Any time graph must define what is being normalized: duration, scale, salience, influence, memory, anticipation, uncertainty, probability, or another explicit quantity.

## 7. Visualization canon

Fibonacci destinations and ratios occur at integer Fv steps. A graph may connect points to show order, but it must not imply uncomputed continuous Fibonacci states. Smooth splines are dangerous because they invent intermediate values, crossings, and extrema.

Every finished graph must state:

1. the question it answers;
2. its domain: exact math, geometry, empirical evidence, metadata/systems, application/practice, metaphor, or combination;
3. the indexing convention and any conversion;
4. data lineage: canonical inventory values, recurrence-computed values, measurements, user data, simulation, or metaphor;
5. all variables, axes, units, encodings, transformations, and reference values;
6. what the viewer should discover;
7. what the graph does **not** prove;
8. relevant audit risks.

Required visual cautions:

- Keep destination plots, forward-ratio plots, reciprocal-ratio plots, signed-error plots, absolute-error plots, and consecutive-pair plots distinct.
- Label axes by actual quantity, not vague words like “Value” or “Index.”
- Mark reference lines by role: `0`, `1`, `phi`, `1/phi`, `y = x`, `y = phi*x`, asymptote, baseline, or competing model.
- Preserve visible markers for exact step observations.
- Do not let late-ratio rounding erase whether a value lies above or below `phi`.
- Use zero baselines for ordinary bar charts; do not put zero on a standard logarithmic axis.
- A linear destination scale to 514229 compresses early values near zero; use insets, log views, or paired panels when needed.
- Equal numeric units require equal pixel height on a linear bar chart unless a transformation or axis break is declared.

## 8. Rendering and screenshot canon

Numerical source data are more authoritative than screenshot measurements. A rendered plot is limited by pixel resolution, line width, marker size, antialiasing, cropping, export scaling, and compression.

Do not claim subpixel differences are visibly resolved. Do not treat a thick line as a single exact row of pixels. Do not use cropped screenshots, captured UI chrome, or low-resolution exports as if they were mathematical content. Inspect rendered artifacts only after source values and coordinate definitions are fixed.

## 9. Geometry and spiral canon

A golden rectangle has aspect ratio `phi` or `1/phi` depending on orientation. A Fibonacci square has equal width and height, with side length equal to a Fibonacci destination used in the construction.

A square-based Fibonacci spiral approximation uses quarter-circle arcs:

- each square must remain a true square;
- side lengths follow `1, 1, 2, 3, 5, 8, 13, ...`;
- each arc radius equals the side length of its containing square;
- each arc center lies at the correct square corner;
- adjacent arcs meet tangentially when layout and centers are correct;
- recursive square attachment must continue the construction;
- uniform scaling must preserve geometry.

A Fibonacci spiral approximation is piecewise quarter-circle geometry. An exact golden spiral is logarithmic. They are related visually but not identical.

## 10. Evidence-boundary canon

The repository's core discipline is to make truth visible without changing its kind. Always separate:

- exact identities;
- recurrence-generated consequences;
- finite values;
- asymptotic limits;
- rounded decimals;
- rendered pixels;
- empirical measurements;
- statistical correlations;
- visual resemblance;
- historical/design claims;
- speculative interpretations;
- metaphors.

For plants, shells, bodies, markets, architecture, art, planning, behavior, or time, never infer a Fibonacci mechanism from resemblance or numerical nearness alone. Compare alternatives where possible, use residuals, show uncertainty and sample size, and state what evidence would distinguish the claim from non-Fibonacci explanations.

The correct question is not “Can Fibonacci be seen here?” The correct question is: “What claim is being made, what evidence would distinguish it from alternatives, and what kind of truth does the evidence support?”

## 11. Known-from-known canon

A recurring philosophical frame is that later Fibonacci destinations are implicit once the seeds and recurrence rule are fixed. In exact recurrence work, future terms are determined by known prior destinations and the rule.

Use this only within its proper boundary. Do not generalize recurrence determinism into claims that real events are predetermined, predictable, or caused by Fibonacci structure. Separate formal dependency from physical causation, forecasting, metaphysics, and metaphor.

Guiding question:

> What future structure is already implicit in the present rule, state, ratio, boundary, or normalization?

## 12. Graph-suite canon

The curated graph suite is authoritative as a working reference, expandable, revisable, nonexclusive, subordinate to truth/avoid standards, and intended for selection, combination, critique, and invention.

Choose the smallest visualization set that reveals the needed structure. Prefer graphs that expose recurrence, dependency, convergence, scale, symmetry, error, invariance, transformation, evidence boundary, or interpretive contrast. Reject novelty, redundant variants, decorative charts, and visuals that blur exactness, approximation, evidence, and metaphor.

Major graph families include:

- destination value graphs;
- recurrence/dependency graphs;
- ratio and convergence graphs;
- exact identity graphs;
- geometry and spiral graphs;
- empirical/reality-test graphs;
- metadata/systems graphs;
- normalized-time metaphor graphs.

A new graph candidate must state its name, central question, variables, visual form, insight, warnings, and category. Cosmetic renaming does not earn canon status.

## 13. Avoid taxonomy compressed canon

The 100 AV entries compress into these audit domains:

1. indexing, labels, terminology, convention mixing, omitted zero, merged duplicate one, count confusion, undefined ratios;
2. seed values, recurrence arithmetic, object counts, source/result arrows, typos, substitution, partial verification, endpoint omissions;
3. ratio reversal, false exactness, wrong time-reference placement, monotonicity errors, error/swing confusion, omitted limit/recurrence, scale collision, rounding-sign loss;
4. chart construction: splines, missing markers, vague axes, missing units, clipping, clutter, log-axis zero, orderless scatter, missing reference lines, inconsistent scales;
5. pixel/rendering: manual nudging, subpixel claims, line-width and antialiasing errors, screenshot overtrust, scale drift, crop, UI capture, export assumptions;
6. rectangles and spirals: false golden rectangles, nonsquare tiles, wrong side lengths, wrong arc radii or centers, broken tangency, decorative/freehand spirals, nonuniform resizing;
7. explanation/document integrity: ambiguous legends, color-only meaning, missing convention/formulas/source values, copied errors, panel disagreement, overclaiming, incomplete audit.

When auditing, classify every problem by AV domain and support every correction by TR/REF values where possible.

## 14. Boot-up prompt skeleton

A strong future boot-up prompt should require the assistant to:

1. read [`the_essences.md`](the_essences.md) first;
2. re-read [`02_Fibonacci_Truth_Inventory.docx`](02_Fibonacci_Truth_Inventory.docx) and [`01_Fibonacci_Avoids_and_Error_Inventory.docx`](01_Fibonacci_Avoids_and_Error_Inventory.docx) before any substantive claim;
3. re-read [`insightgraphsuite.md`](insightgraphsuite.md) and [`draw.md`](draw.md) before any graphing or visual-design work;
4. state the source hierarchy and Fv convention back in working notes;
5. load the canonical Fv -1 through Fv 28 destination table;
6. keep Fv step, `D_v` destination, conventional index, transition, ratio, reciprocal, finite value, limit, rounded decimal, rendered pixel, empirical observation, and metaphor separate;
7. use the Truth Inventory for positive facts and the Avoids Inventory for failure labels;
8. consult the graph suite only after the mathematical convention and claim type are fixed;
9. avoid overclaiming, decorative graphing, unmarked approximation, and external Fibonacci folklore unless evidence boundaries are explicit;
10. produce outputs that preserve exactness, clarity, humility, and auditability.

## 15. Final maxim

Start from convention. Separate step from destination. Protect exactness. Mark approximation. Respect limits. Audit visuals. Bound metaphors. Test external claims. Make the specific truth visible without pretending it is a different kind of truth.
