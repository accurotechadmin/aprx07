## Ground-up purpose

This document is a new, intentionally unbalanced expansion layer. It does **not** normalize the repository back into equal section lengths. It begins from small artifacts of Fibonacci knowledge, pauses over each one, asks why it matters, and turns the extracted insight into recommended graphs, images, figures, `.md` references, `.json` manifests, and `.xlsx` workbooks.

The goal is not symmetry of coverage. The goal is extraction: squeeze every useful drop from each seed of knowledge so future sessions can decide which ideas deserve canon, SSOT, reference, worksheet, figure, or audit status.

## How to read this document

Each entry has these parts:

- **Artifact of knowledge:** the small thing being pressed.
- **Why it matters:** what becomes visible when the artifact is respected.
- **Reflection questions:** prompts for slowing down and extracting more meaning.
- **Recommended graphs and figures:** especially `.svg` artifacts.
- **Recommended documents and data:** `.md`, `.json`, and `.xlsx` artifacts.
- **Maturity:** elementary, graduated, advanced, or transcendent-return.
- **Truth boundary:** exact math, computed consequence, geometry, metadata model, empirical test, or metaphor.

The four maturity labels are intentionally practical:

1. **Elementary:** discover the apparently unknown by carrying out the recurrence, naming the step, and watching the next value become known.
2. **Graduated:** infer order, direction, ratios, errors, dependencies, and possible continuations from any known point or local sequence state.
3. **Advanced:** navigate alternatives, transforms, identities, visual encodings, empirical tests, and metaphorical extensions without category errors.
4. **Transcendent-return:** diverge safely into meta thought, time thought, life metaphor, systems metaphor, or creative analogy, then return to exactness, evidence, and resolution.

## Pressed knowledge recommendations

### OP-001 — The zero seed is not emptiness; it is addressable beginning

**Artifact of knowledge:** `Fv -1 -> destination 0`.

**Why it matters:** The visible zero prevents a false origin. It teaches that nothingness can have an address in a process without being a positive destination. It also prevents the sequence from being re-indexed into a prettier but less truthful shape.

**Reflection questions:** What changes when zero is shown rather than hidden? How does a process remember that it started before the first visible one? What kinds of documents need a visible null, baseline, or prior state?

**Recommended graphs and figures:**

- `drawings/zero_seed_as_address.svg` — a one-panel figure showing Fv -1 as a valid address with destination 0.
- `drawings/visible_seed_triplet.svg` — a seed diagram showing 0, 1, 1 as three distinct process positions.
- `drawings/zero_omission_failure_contrast.svg` — before/after chart comparing correct Fv labels with a zero-omitted version.

**Recommended documents and data:**

- `zero_seed_reflection.md` — a conceptual note on why a zero can be structurally active without being a positive count.
- `seed_triplet_manifest.json` — machine-readable seed definitions and forbidden simplifications.
- `zero_seed_indexing_workbook.xlsx` — conversion examples between Fv and conventional indexing.

**Maturity:** elementary.

**Truth boundary:** exact repository convention and indexing discipline.

### OP-002 — The duplicate one is not redundancy; it is a hinge

**Artifact of knowledge:** `Fv 0 -> 1` and `Fv 1 -> 1` are distinct steps with equal destinations.

**Why it matters:** The repeated one is where equality and sequence order diverge. It is the first lesson that a value alone is not enough; a value must keep its address, history, and role.

**Reflection questions:** When do two identical values mean two different things? What does the second `1` allow that the first alone cannot? How many repository errors arise from merging them?

**Recommended graphs and figures:**

- `drawings/duplicate_one_hinge.svg` — two equal values drawn as distinct process nodes.
- `drawings/value_identity_vs_step_identity.svg` — same destination, different step identity.
- `drawings/merged_one_error_map.svg` — how merging the duplicate one shifts every downstream label.

**Recommended documents and data:**

- `duplicate_one_hinge.md` — explanatory document on value equality versus process identity.
- `duplicate_one_audit_manifest.json` — checks for omitted, merged, or mislabeled duplicate-one presentations.
- `seed_identity_examples.xlsx` — tables where values repeat but process addresses remain distinct.

**Maturity:** elementary.

**Truth boundary:** exact convention and avoid taxonomy.

### OP-003 — Addition is a way of making the future stop being mysterious

**Artifact of knowledge:** `D_v = D_(v-1) + D_(v-2)`.

**Why it matters:** The recurrence converts a future destination into a consequence of known prior destinations. The educational drama is to pretend the next value is unknown, then let it become known by rule.

**Reflection questions:** What was unknown before the addition? What was already implicit? How can a graph preserve the feeling of discovery while still admitting determinism under the recurrence rule?

**Recommended graphs and figures:**

**Recommended documents and data:**

- `recurrence_reveal_steps.json` — ordered reveal states for animated or static figures.
- `recurrence_stage_workbook.xlsx` — formulas that leave the next cell blank until computed.

**Maturity:** elementary.

**Truth boundary:** exact recurrence-generated consequence.

**Artifact of knowledge:** thirty destinations contain twenty-nine adjacent transitions.

**Why it matters:** Count confusion is one of the smallest ways to fracture the entire document system. A destination is a place; a transition is a relation between places.

**Reflection questions:** What does a transition know that a destination does not? How many arrows are needed to connect thirty nodes? When should a workbook count rows versus gaps?

- `destination_transition_workbook.xlsx` — formulas counting destinations, gaps, ratios, and undefined cases.

**Maturity:** elementary to graduated.

**Truth boundary:** exact finite structure and labeling discipline.

### OP-005 — Ratio is a view of relation, not a destination value

**Artifact of knowledge:** `R_v = D_v / D_(v-1)` and `Q_v = D_(v-1) / D_v`.

**Why it matters:** Ratio is a relationship between adjacent destinations. It should never be confused with a term, a destination, or the limit itself.

**Reflection questions:** Which two destinations are being compared? Which direction is the comparison facing? What changes if the numerator and denominator reverse?

- `ratio_direction_workbook.xlsx` — side-by-side forward/reciprocal calculations.

**Maturity:** graduated.

**Truth boundary:** exact finite rational relation and convergence setup.

## Canon-candidate reflection axes

Future expansion should not merely ask "what graph can be drawn?" It should ask which axis of truth is being reflected:

1. **Address:** Where is the thing in the process?
2. **Destination:** What value is reached there?
3. **Source pair:** What two prior values make it known?
4. **Transition:** What relation connects adjacent destinations?
5. **Ratio direction:** Which way does the quotient face?
6. **Limit:** What is approached but not reached finitely?
7. **Error:** What remains between finite approximation and limit?
8. **Scale:** What does the chosen visual transformation reveal or hide?
9. **Construction:** What geometric rule makes the figure valid?
10. **Evidence:** What distinguishes claim from resemblance?
11. **Metadata:** What document or record depends on what other document or record?
12. **Normalization:** What is being placed into a shared coordinate system?
13. **Divergence:** Where did exactness branch into interpretation?
14. **Return:** What audit brings the branch back to resolution?

## Final expansion principle

The repository has reached a meaningful level of normalization. This document recommends a deliberate next phase of uneven growth. Some branches should become much longer than others because they contain more juice right now. That imbalance is not a defect during extraction. It becomes a defect only if later canonization forgets to mark sources, truth boundaries, evidence standards, and return paths.
